Tiger's Nest Monastery

Perched dramatically on a cliffside 3,120 meters above sea level, this sacred monastery is Bhutan's most iconic site.

Hike: 4-5 hours round trip

Best time: October-December or March-April

Punakha Dzong

The "Palace of Great Happiness" is arguably Bhutan's most beautiful dzong, situated at the confluence of two rivers.

History: Built in 1637-38

Feature: Houses sacred relics

Thimphu

Bhutan's charming capital is the world's only capital city without traffic lights, preserving traditional architecture.

Must-see: Buddha Dordenma, Memorial Chorten

Unique: No traffic lights

Dochula Pass

This mountain pass offers stunning 360-degree Himalayan views and features 108 memorial chortens.

Elevation: 3,100 meters

View: Himalayan peaks on clear days

Phobjikha Valley

A beautiful glacial valley and important wildlife preserve, winter home to endangered black-necked cranes.

Wildlife: Black-necked cranes (Oct-Feb)

Activities: Nature walks, crane viewing

Bhutanese Culture

Experience vibrant festivals (tsechus) with masked dances, traditional architecture, and distinctive national dress.

Dress: Gho for men, Kira for women

Festivals: Paro and Thimphu Tsechu

Bhutan Wisdom

"Bhutan measures prosperity through Gross National Happiness rather than GDP. This philosophy permeates every aspect of life in the Last Shangri-La, where ancient Buddhist traditions coexist with careful modernization. From the prayer flags fluttering in mountain passes to the vibrant festivals celebrating spiritual heritage, Bhutan offers a journey not just through spectacular landscapes, but into a different way of being."

Essential Travel Tips

Visa Requirements

All tourists (except India, Bangladesh, Maldives) must book through licensed tour operators.

Daily Tariff

$200-250 per day includes accommodation, meals, guide, transportation and sustainable development fee.

Best Seasons

March-May and September-November for pleasant weather and clear mountain views.

Cultural Etiquette

Dress modestly, avoid public displays of affection, always circumambate religious sites clockwise.
