Belém Tower

Iconic 16th-century fortress on the Tagus River, a masterpiece of Manueline architecture and UNESCO site.

Nearby: Try Pastéis de Belém

Admission: €6

Tram 28

Iconic yellow tram winding through Alfama, Graça, and Baixa districts - the best sightseeing route.

Tip: Go early to avoid crowds

Ticket: €3 single ride

São Jorge Castle

Moorish castle offering panoramic views over Lisbon's red rooftops and the Tagus River.

Best for: Sunset views

Admission: €10

Jerónimos Monastery

UNESCO-listed masterpiece of Manueline architecture where Vasco da Gama is buried.

Must-see: Cloisters

Admission: €10

Alfama District

Lisbon's oldest quarter with medieval streets, fado houses, and the Sé Cathedral.

Best for: Evening fado performances

Don't miss: Miradouro viewpoints

LX Factory

Hip creative hub in a former industrial complex with shops, restaurants and street art.

Must-visit: Ler Devagar bookstore

Best day: Sunday market

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Walkable but hilly - use trams/elevators for steep climbs. Metro is efficient for longer distances.

Money Matters

Euro (€) is currency. Credit cards widely accepted but carry cash for small shops/trams.

Weather

300+ sunny days/year. Summers hot (25-35°C), winters mild (8-15°C). Always bring comfortable shoes.

Language

Portuguese is main language but English widely spoken in tourist areas. Learn "Obrigado/a" (Thank you).
