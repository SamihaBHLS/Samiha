Experience Amazing Bangkok

Where ancient temples meet modern skyscrapers and bustling street markets

Grand Palace

Spectacular complex of buildings including Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha).

Must-see: The Emerald Buddha in the ordination hall

Dress code: Cover shoulders and knees

Wat Arun

Stunning "Temple of Dawn" with Khmer-style prang decorated with colorful porcelain.

Best time: Sunset for magical views

Tip: Take the ferry from Tha Tien pier

Chatuchak Market

World's largest weekend market with over 15,000 stalls selling everything imaginable.

Must-try: Coconut ice cream in a coconut shell

Best time: Early morning to avoid heat/crowds

Khao San Road

Famous backpacker street with cheap hostels, street food, bars, and shopping.

Nearby: Soi Rambuttri for slightly quieter vibe

Tip: Try fried scorpions if you're adventurous

Chao Phraya River

Bangkok's "River of Kings" with scenic boat rides and riverside attractions.

Must-do: Dinner cruise at sunset

Best way: Hop-on-hop-off tourist boat

Jim Thompson House

Beautiful traditional Thai house museum of the American silk entrepreneur.

Experience: Guided tour of the house and gardens

Alternative: Visit the excellent silk shop

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

BTS Skytrain and MRT subway are efficient. Tuk-tuks for short distances. Grab (like Uber) works well.

Money Matters

Thai Baht (THB) is the currency. Carry small bills for street vendors. ATMs widely available.

Weather

Hot year-round. Cool season (Nov-Feb) best for visiting. Rainy season (Jul-Oct) has heavy showers.

Etiquette

Dress modestly at temples. Don't touch heads or point feet at people. Remove shoes when required.
