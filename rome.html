Experience Timeless Rome

Where ancient ruins, Renaissance art, and la dolce vita come together

The Colosseum

The iconic amphitheater that hosted gladiator battles and public spectacles in ancient Rome.

Tip: Buy combo ticket with Roman Forum

Hours: 8:30AM-7PM daily

Vatican City

The world's smallest independent state, home to St. Peter's Basilica and the Vatican Museums.

Must-see: Sistine Chapel ceiling

Dress code: Covered shoulders/knees

Trevi Fountain

Rome's most famous Baroque fountain, where tossing a coin ensures your return to Rome.

Best time: Early morning to avoid crowds

Legend: Throw with right hand over left shoulder

The Pantheon

Best-preserved ancient Roman temple with the world's largest unreinforced concrete dome.

Free entry: Open to public

Buried here: Raphael and Italian kings

Roman Forum

The heart of ancient Rome with ruins of government buildings, temples, and markets.

Combo ticket: With Colosseum and Palatine Hill

Guides: Recommended to understand the ruins

Trastevere

Charming medieval neighborhood across the Tiber with authentic restaurants and nightlife.

Must-do: Evening passeggiata (stroll)

Try: Cacio e pepe pasta

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Metro, buses, and trams cover most areas. Consider Roma Pass for transport and museum discounts.

Money Matters

Euro (€) is the currency. Cash preferred in smaller establishments. ATMs widely available.

Weather

Hot summers (30°C+), mild winters. Spring/fall ideal. Always carry water in summer.

Language

Italian is official but English widely understood in tourist areas. Learn basic greetings like "Buongiorno."
