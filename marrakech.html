Experience Exotic Marrakech

Where ancient medinas meet luxurious riads under the Atlas Mountains

Jemaa el-Fnaa

The pulsating heart of Marrakech with snake charmers, storytellers, and food stalls by day transforming into a massive open-air restaurant at night.

Must-try: Fresh orange juice and snail soup

Best time: Sunset for the full spectacle

Bahia Palace

19th-century palace showcasing the finest Moroccan craftsmanship with intricate tilework, carved cedar ceilings, and lush gardens.

Hours: 9AM-5PM daily

Admission: 70 MAD

Souks of Marrakech

Labyrinthine markets selling spices, leather, lanterns, and carpets - follow the scent trails of cumin, saffron and cedar.

Must-buy: Argan oil, babouches, ceramics

Tip: Bargain starting at 1/3 asking price

Majorelle Garden

Yves Saint Laurent's botanical oasis with cobalt blue buildings, exotic plants, and the Berber Museum.

Hours: 8AM-6PM

Nearby: YSL Museum

Atlas Mountains

Day trip to Berber villages with stunning valleys and waterfalls, or multi-day treks to Mount Toubkal.

Popular tour: Ourika Valley

Distance: 1-2 hours from city

Moroccan Hammam

Traditional steam bath experience with black soap scrub and ghassoul clay treatment - essential Marrakech relaxation.

Luxury option: La Mamounia spa

Local experience: Neighborhood hammam

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Medina is walkable, use petit taxis (beige) for longer distances. Agree on fares first.

Money Matters

Moroccan Dirham (MAD) is the currency. ATMs widely available. Bargain in souks.

Weather

Hot summers (35°C+), mild winters (20°C). Spring/fall ideal. Evenings can be cool.

Dress Code

Modest clothing recommended (cover shoulders/knees), especially for women.
