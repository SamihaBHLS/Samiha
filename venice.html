Experience the Romance of Venice

Gondolas, grand palaces, and winding canals await you

St. Mark's Square

The heart of Venice, surrounded by stunning architecture including St. Mark's Basilica and the Campanile.

Best time to visit: Early morning to avoid crowds

Must-see: The golden mosaics inside the Basilica

Grand Canal

The main waterway of Venice, lined with magnificent palaces and crossed by the Rialto Bridge.

Best way to see: Vaporetto (water bus) Line 1

Tip: Ride at sunset for magical views

Rialto Bridge

The oldest bridge across the Grand Canal, offering spectacular views and bustling markets nearby.

Best for: Photography and shopping

Note: Very crowded during midday

Doge's Palace

A masterpiece of Gothic architecture, once the residence of Venice's rulers and seat of government.

Hours: 8:30 AM - 7:00 PM (summer)

Admission: €25 (includes Bridge of Sighs)

Murano Island

Famous for its centuries-old glass-making tradition, with demonstrations and beautiful glass art.

Try: Watch a glass-blowing demonstration

How to get there: Vaporetto lines 4.1, 4.2, or 3

Gondola Ride

The quintessential Venetian experience, gliding through picturesque canals with a singing gondolier.

Price: €80-€120 for 30-40 minutes

Best time: Early evening for sunset views

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Walk or use vaporettos (water buses). Gondolas are expensive but worth it for a special experience.

Best Time to Visit

April-May and September-October for pleasant weather and fewer crowds.

What to Wear

Comfortable walking shoes (lots of bridges) and layers for changing weather.

Photography Tips

Best light is early morning. Get lost in side streets for authentic Venetian scenes.
