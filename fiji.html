Mamanuca Islands

A stunning archipelago of 20 islands known for palm-fringed beaches, coral reefs, and luxury resorts. Popular for snorkeling, diving, and surfing.

Highlights: Monuriki Island (Cast Away filming location)

Activities: Snorkeling, surfing, island hopping

Yasawa Islands

A chain of volcanic islands with dramatic landscapes, pristine beaches, and traditional Fijian villages. Less developed than the Mamanucas.

Must-see: Sawa-i-Lau Caves with underwater passages

Known for: Blue Lagoon, spectacular sunsets

Coral Coast

Viti Levu's famous coastline stretching 80km along the Queen's Road. Features resorts, traditional villages, and the world's fourth-largest barrier reef.

Activities: Shark diving, zip-lining, cultural tours

Highlight: Sigatoka Sand Dunes National Park

Taveuni - Garden Island

Known as the 'Garden Island' for its lush rainforests, waterfalls, and abundant flora. Home to Bouma National Heritage Park and rare bird species.

Unique feature: International Date Line runs through

Must-see: Tavoro Waterfalls, Lavena Coastal Walk

Fijian Culture

Warm, welcoming culture centered around community, tradition, and ceremony. Known for meke dances, lovo feasts, and the welcoming 'Bula!' spirit.

Ceremony: Kava drinking (yaqona)

Cuisine: Kokoda, lovo, tropical fruits

Diving & Snorkeling

Fiji's 'Soft Coral Capital of the World' offers spectacular dive sites with vibrant coral gardens, sharks, manta rays, and incredible marine biodiversity.

Top sites: Great Astrolabe Reef, Rainbow Reef

Season: Year-round (best April-October)

Essential Travel Tips

Visa Requirements

Most nationalities receive a 4-month visa on arrival. Passport must be valid for 6 months beyond stay.

Best Seasons

Dry season: May-October (cooler, less humid). Wet season: November-April (warmer, possible cyclones).

Cultural Etiquette

Dress modestly in villages. Remove hats and sunglasses when entering homes. Participate respectfully in kava ceremonies.

Currency & Costs

Fijian Dollar (FJD). Resorts accept cards but carry cash for villages. Tipping isn't customary but appreciated.
