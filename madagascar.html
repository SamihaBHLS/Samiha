Avenue of the Baobabs

This striking landscape features a dirt road lined with massive baobab trees, some over 800 years old, creating one of Africa's most photographed scenes.

Best time: Sunset for spectacular photos

Location: Morondava, western Madagascar

Andasibe-Mantadia National Park

Home to the indri, Madagascar's largest lemur, this rainforest park offers incredible wildlife viewing just a few hours from the capital.

Highlight: Indri lemurs' haunting calls

Species: 11 lemur species, rare birds

Nosy Be

Madagascar's premier beach destination with pristine white sand, turquoise waters, and excellent diving spots surrounded by smaller islands.

Activities: Diving, snorkeling, whale watching

Known for: Ylang-ylang plantations

Tsingy de Bemaraha

A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring spectacular limestone formations creating a "forest" of sharp peaks, canyons, and suspended bridges.

Unique feature: Karst limestone landscape

Access: April to November only

Ranomafana National Park

A misty rainforest park with hot springs, waterfalls, and incredible biodiversity including 12 species of lemurs and numerous endemic birds.

Specialty: Golden bamboo lemur

Feature: Natural hot springs

Malagasy Culture

A rich blend of Southeast Asian, African, Arab and French influences expressed through music, dance, cuisine, and unique funeral traditions.

Famadihana: Turning of the bones ceremony

Cuisine: Rice-based dishes with unique flavors

Madagascar Wisdom

"Madagascar is a world apart, where evolution took a different path, creating unique wildlife found nowhere else on Earth. From the iconic lemurs and bizarre baobabs to the intricate tapestry of cultures blending Southeast Asian, African, and French influences, this island nation offers an experience like no other. Travel here requires patience and flexibility, but rewards with unparalleled natural wonders and the warmth of the Malagasy people who believe that 'foreigners are gifts from God.'"

Essential Travel Tips

Visa Requirements

Most nationalities need a visa, available on arrival for tourism (approx. $37 for 30 days).

Health Precautions

Malaria prophylaxis recommended. Vaccinations for hepatitis A, typhoid advised.

Best Seasons

April-October (dry season) for best weather. November-March is rainy season with cyclone risk.

Transportation

Domestic flights save time. Roads are often challenging - allow extra travel time for overland journeys.
