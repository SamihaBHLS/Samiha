Experience Dynamic Seoul

Where 600-year-old palaces stand beside futuristic skyscrapers and K-pop culture

Gyeongbokgung Palace

The largest and most beautiful of Seoul's five grand Joseon dynasty palaces, with its iconic Gwanghwamun gate.

Must-see: Changing of the guard ceremony

Tip: Rent hanbok for free admission

Bukchon Hanok Village

Traditional Korean hanok houses nestled between palaces with winding alleys and tea houses.

Best time: Early morning for photos

Note: Respect residents' privacy

N Seoul Tower

Iconic observation tower on Namsan Mountain offering panoramic city views and love locks.

How to reach: Cable car or hike

Best time: Sunset for night views

Myeongdong

Seoul's premier shopping district packed with cosmetics shops, street food, and department stores.

Must-try: Korean street food

Tip: Bring cash for street vendors

Korean BBQ

Interactive dining experience grilling premium meats at your table with endless banchan (side dishes).

Best cuts: Samgyeopsal (pork belly) or galbi (beef ribs)

Pair with: Soju or makgeolli

DMZ Tour

Visit the heavily fortified border with North Korea, including the Joint Security Area (JSA).

Requires: Advance booking with tour

Duration: Half-day trip from Seoul

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Excellent subway system (buy T-money card). Taxis are affordable (orange/silver most common).

Money Matters

Korean Won (KRW) is the currency. Credit cards widely accepted but carry cash for markets.

Weather

Hot humid summers, cold winters. Spring (cherry blossoms) and fall (foliage) are ideal.

Language

Korean is official but English signs common. Learn basic phrases like "Annyeonghaseyo" (Hello).
