Experience Vibrant Mumbai

Where Bollywood glamour meets colonial heritage and street food delights

Gateway of India

Iconic 20th-century arch monument overlooking the Arabian Sea, built during British rule.

Must-do: Boat ride to Elephanta Caves

Best time: Early morning or sunset

Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus

UNESCO-listed Victorian Gothic railway station and historic hub of Mumbai's rail network.

Architecture: Blend of Victorian and Indian styles

Tip: Visit during golden hour

Marine Drive

3.6km crescent-shaped boulevard along the coast, famous for its art deco buildings.

Must-see: Nighttime "Queen's Necklace" lights

Best for: Evening strolls

Elephanta Caves

UNESCO World Heritage site with 5th-8th century rock-cut cave temples dedicated to Shiva.

Access: 1-hour ferry from Gateway

Features: Massive carved sculptures

Dharavi

One of Asia's largest slums with thriving small-scale industries and community spirit.

Tour: Responsible guided tours available

Produces: Leather, pottery, recycling

Juhu Beach

Popular beach destination famous for its street food, especially bhelpuri and pav bhaji.

Best time: Early morning or evening

Nearby: Bollywood star homes

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Local trains are fastest but crowded. Uber/Ola work well. Metered taxis available.

Money Matters

Indian Rupee (₹) is the currency. Carry cash for small purchases and street food.

Best Seasons

Nov-Feb (cooler) best for sightseeing. Avoid monsoon (Jun-Sep) due to floods.

Street Food

Try vada pav, pav bhaji, bhelpuri. Choose busy stalls with high turnover.
