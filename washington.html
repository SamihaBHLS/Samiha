Experience Iconic Washington DC

Where monuments to democracy meet vibrant neighborhoods and world-class museums

National Mall

The two-mile park stretching from the Lincoln Memorial to the Capitol, lined with Smithsonian museums and iconic monuments.

Must see: Lincoln Memorial Reflecting Pool

Tip: Best visited at sunrise or sunset

Smithsonian Museums

19 world-class museums and galleries (plus the National Zoo) offering free admission to all visitors.

Top picks: Air & Space, Natural History

Hidden gem: National Portrait Gallery

The White House

The official residence and workplace of the U.S. president, a symbol of American democracy since 1800.

Tour: Request through your Member of Congress

Best view: From Lafayette Square

U.S. Capitol

Home of the U.S. Congress with its iconic dome, featuring historic art and architecture.

Must do: Free guided tour

Don't miss: Capitol Rotunda paintings

Georgetown

Historic neighborhood with cobblestone streets, upscale shopping, and waterfront dining.

Must visit: Georgetown Waterfront Park

Iconic: Exorcist Steps at 36th & Prospect

National Arboretum

446-acre living museum with gardens, bonsai collection, and the original U.S. Capitol columns.

Highlight: National Bonsai & Penjing Museum

Best season: Cherry blossoms in spring

It's a city designed to inspire, with free access to some of the world's greatest treasures."

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Use Metro (subway) or Capital Bikeshare. The Circulator bus is great for tourists.

Museum Visits

Smithsonian museums are free. Timed-entry passes needed for some (like African American History).

Best Seasons

Spring (cherry blossoms) and fall (pleasant weather). Summer can be hot and humid.

Neighborhoods

Explore beyond the Mall: U Street jazz clubs, Eastern Market, Dupont Circle bookstores.
