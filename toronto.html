Experience Vibrant Toronto

Where urban sophistication meets natural beauty and multicultural charm

CN Tower

Iconic 553m telecommunications tower with glass floor and EdgeWalk experience.

Must-do: 360 Restaurant with rotating views

Best time: Sunset for spectacular city lights

Royal Ontario Museum

Canada's largest museum with world cultures, natural history, and striking architecture.

Highlight: The stunning Michael Lee-Chin Crystal

Tip: Free admission for under 14s

Toronto Islands

Car-free island chain with beaches, parks, and the best skyline views of Toronto.

Must-try: Bike rental to explore the islands

Best time: Summer for beach activities

Distillery District

Victorian-era industrial district now housing art galleries, restaurants, and boutiques.

Nearby: Christmas Market in winter

Tip: Try craft beer at Mill Street Brewery

High Park

Toronto's largest public park with zoo, gardens, trails, and cherry blossoms in spring.

Must-see: Sakura cherry blossoms in May

Activity: Paddle boating on Grenadier Pond

St. Lawrence Market

Historic food market since 1803, named world's best food market by National Geographic.

Experience: Peameal bacon sandwich at Carousel Bakery

Alternative: Saturday farmer's market

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

TTC subway/streetcars are efficient. Presto card saves money. Bike share available.

Money Matters

Canadian Dollar (CAD) is the currency. Tipping 15-20% expected in restaurants.

Weather

Summer (Jun-Aug) warm (20-30°C). Winter (Dec-Feb) cold (-10 to 0°C).

Safety

Generally safe. Standard city precautions apply in downtown areas at night.
