Where Byzantine grandeur meets Ottoman splendor and modern vibrancy

Hagia Sophia

The architectural marvel that served as church, mosque, and now museum spanning 1500 years of history.

Must-see: Massive dome and Byzantine mosaics

Tip: Visit early to avoid crowds

Blue Mosque

Official name Sultanahmet Mosque, famous for its six minarets and 20,000+ blue Iznik tiles.

Note: Closed during prayer times

Dress code: Modest clothing required

Grand Bazaar

One of the world's oldest and largest covered markets with over 4,000 shops.

Find: Carpets, lamps, spices, jewelry

Tip: Bargain politely but firmly

Bosphorus Cruise

Boat tour between continents passing palaces, fortresses and waterfront mansions.

Best time: Sunset cruise

Alternative: Public ferry to Kadıköy

Topkapi Palace

Ottoman sultans' lavish residence with harem, treasury and holy relics.

Must-see: Imperial Treasury and Harem

Hours: 9AM-6PM (closed Tuesdays)

Turkish Cuisine

From kebabs to mezes, baklava to Turkish coffee - a feast for all senses.

Must-try: Testi kebab, manti, kunefe

Drink: Apple tea or raki

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Use Istanbulkart for trams, metro, ferries. Taxis are affordable but ensure meter is on.

Money Matters

Turkish Lira (TRY) is the currency. Credit cards widely accepted but carry some cash.

Weather

Hot summers (June-Aug), cool winters. Spring and fall are ideal with mild temperatures.

Language

Turkish is official but English spoken in tourist areas. Learn basic greetings like "Merhaba".
