Experience Enchanting Edinburgh

Where ancient castles, literary legends, and whisky warmth create a timeless Scottish experience

Edinburgh Castle

Iconic fortress towering over the city, home to Scotland's crown jewels and the Stone of Destiny.

Must-see: One O'Clock Gun

Admission: £18.50 adults

Royal Mile

Historic thoroughfare connecting Edinburgh Castle to Holyrood Palace, lined with shops and landmarks.

Don't miss: The Real Mary King's Close

Best for: Souvenirs and whisky

Arthur's Seat

Ancient volcano offering panoramic city views, perfect for a moderate hike in Holyrood Park.

Duration: 1-2 hour hike

Best time: Sunrise or sunset

Scotch Whisky Experience

Highlight: World's largest whisky collection

Tours: From £20

National Museum of Scotland

Fascinating collections spanning Scottish history, science, technology and world cultures.

Admission: Free (special exhibits extra)

Must-see: Dolly the Sheep

Edinburgh Festival

World's largest arts festival every August, transforming the city into a cultural wonderland.

Includes: Fringe, International, and more

Tip: Book accommodation early

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Compact city center is very walkable. Buses excellent for farther destinations.

Weather

Changeable year-round - always carry layers and a waterproof jacket.

When to Visit

Summer for festivals, winter for Christmas markets, spring/fall for fewer crowds.

Money

British Pound (£). Credit cards widely accepted but carry some cash.
