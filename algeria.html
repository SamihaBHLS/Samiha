Experience Majestic Algeria

Where ancient Roman ruins meet vast Saharan landscapes and Mediterranean coastlines

Tassili n'Ajjer

A vast plateau in the Sahara desert, famous for its prehistoric rock art and unique geological formations.

Best time to visit: October to April

UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1982

Djemila

Well-preserved Roman ruins with a unique adaptation to mountain terrain, featuring temples, basilicas and arches.

Location: Northern Algeria mountains

Name meaning: "Beautiful" in Arabic

Algiers Casbah

The historic medina of Algiers, a unique Islamic city with winding streets, palaces, and mosques overlooking the Mediterranean.

Architecture: Ottoman and Moorish styles

UNESCO Site: Since 1992

Sahara Desert

The world's largest hot desert covers 80% of Algeria, featuring dramatic sand dunes, oases, and unique desert wildlife.

Experience: Camel trekking and desert camping

Highlight: The Grand Erg Oriental dunes

Timgad

A Roman colonial town founded by Emperor Trajan around 100 AD, known for its perfect grid plan and impressive arch.

Nickname: "The Pompeii of Africa"

Special feature: Exceptionally preserved layout

Oran

Algeria's second largest city, known for its Rai music, Spanish-influenced architecture, and vibrant cultural scene.

Famous for: Birthplace of Rai music

Must-see: Santa Cruz fort and Bey's Palace

Algerian Culinary Delights

Couscous

National dish served with vegetables, meat, and broth

Best enjoyed: Friday family meals

Traditional: Mechoui

Whole lamb slow-roasted in underground oven

Specialty: Festival and celebration dish

Street Food: Maaqouda

Potato fritters often served in bread

Perfect for: Quick snack on the go

Sweet: Baklava

Algerian version with almonds and honey

Best with: Traditional mint tea

Algerian Wisdom

Essential Travel Tips for Algeria

Visa Requirements

Most visitors need a visa. Apply through Algerian consulates in advance.

Best Time to Visit

October-April for Sahara, May-September for coastal areas.

Language

Arabic and French are widely spoken. English in tourist areas.

Currency

Algerian Dinar (DZD). Cash is preferred outside major cities.
