Forests are disappearing at an alarming rate due to logging, agriculture, and urban expansion. At current rates, we could lose all rainforests within 100 years. Immediate action is needed to protect these vital ecosystems that regulate our climate, provide clean air and water, and house incredible biodiversity.

Major Threats to Forests

Deforestation

Clearing forests for agriculture, logging, and development removes vital habitat and releases stored carbon.

  • Responsible for 15% of global emissions
  • Palm oil production drives tropical deforestation

Climate Change

Rising temperatures and changing rainfall patterns stress forest ecosystems.

  • Increased wildfires and pest outbreaks
  • Shifting growing zones affect tree species

Mining & Extraction

Resource extraction fragments forests and pollutes waterways.

  • Gold mining mercury poisons rivers
  • Oil drilling brings roads and settlements

Invasive Species

Non-native plants and animals disrupt delicate forest balances.

  • Emerald ash borer killing North American ash
  • Kudzu vine smothers southern US forests

Take Action

Conservation Success Stories

Costa Rica's Reforestation

After having one of the highest deforestation rates in the 1980s, Costa Rica doubled its forest cover to over 50% through strong policies and eco-tourism incentives.

85% forest cover increase since 1987

Atlantic Forest Pact

A coalition of governments, NGOs and companies committed to restore 15 million hectares of Brazil's Atlantic Forest by 2050, protecting endangered species.

45% of target already achieved
