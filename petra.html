Explore Ancient Petra

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World

The Treasury (Al-Khazneh)

The most elaborate temple in Petra, carved into the sandstone cliff face in the 1st century BC as a tomb for an important Nabataean king.

Best time to visit: Early morning for golden light

Fun fact: Featured in Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade

The Monastery (Ad-Deir)

One of the largest monuments in Petra, reached by climbing 800 steps cut into the mountain, offering breathtaking views.

Height: 50 meters wide and 45 meters tall

Tip: Allow 1-2 hours for the climb

The Siq

The main entrance to Petra is a narrow, winding canyon over 1km long with towering walls up to 80 meters high.

Features: Remains of ancient dams and carvings

Experience: Dramatic approach to the Treasury

Royal Tombs

A collection of impressive tomb facades including the Urn Tomb, Silk Tomb, Corinthian Tomb, and Palace Tomb.

Location: Opposite the theater

Best for: Sunset views over Petra

High Place of Sacrifice

One of the highest points in Petra, used for religious ceremonies with altars for animal sacrifices and stunning panoramic views.

Trail: Steep climb but well-preserved path

View: Overlooks entire Petra basin

Petra by Night

A magical experience where the Siq and Treasury are illuminated by over 1,500 candles, with traditional Bedouin music.

When: Monday, Wednesday and Thursday

Tip: Book in advance as it sells out

Petra Wisdom

"Half as old as time itself, Petra stands as a testament to human ingenuity. A rose-red city carved directly into the living rock, it whispers stories of ancient Nabataeans who turned this desert stronghold into a crossroads of civilizations. Essential Travel Tips

What to Wear

Comfortable walking shoes are essential. Dress modestly out of respect for local culture.

Weather Prep

Desert climate - hot days, cool nights. Bring sun protection and layers for temperature changes.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November) for pleasant temperatures.

Tickets & Guides

Multi-day tickets available. Consider a local guide to fully appreciate the history.
