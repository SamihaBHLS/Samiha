Experience Magical Prague

Where Gothic spires meet Bohemian charm along the Vltava River

Prague Castle

The largest ancient castle complex in the world, overlooking the city with stunning views.

Must-see: St. Vitus Cathedral and Golden Lane

Best time: Early morning to avoid crowds

Charles Bridge

The iconic 14th-century stone bridge adorned with Baroque statues, connecting Old Town to Lesser Town.

Tip: Visit at sunrise for magical atmosphere

Look for: Statue of St. John of Nepomuk

Old Town Square

The heart of Prague with colorful Baroque buildings, the Astronomical Clock, and Týn Church.

Must-see: Astronomical Clock show every hour

Best for: Christmas markets in winter

Jewish Quarter (Josefov)

Historic area with synagogues and the Old Jewish Cemetery, one of Europe's most important Jewish heritage sites.

Must-visit: Spanish Synagogue

Admission: Combined ticket available

Petřín Hill

Green oasis with observation tower, mirror maze, and the best panoramic views of Prague.

How to get there: Funicular from Újezd

Best time: Sunset for breathtaking views

Beer Culture

Prague is the birthplace of Pilsner with legendary breweries and beer halls throughout the city.

Must-try: Pilsner Urquell or Budvar

Best spots: U Fleků or Lokál pubs

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Excellent public transport (metro, trams, buses). Get a Prague Card for unlimited travel.

Money Matters

Czech Koruna (CZK) is the currency. Many places accept cards but have cash for smaller shops.

Weather

Variable - warm summers (20-30°C), cold winters with possible snow. Spring and fall are lovely.

Language

Czech is the official language but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.
