Experience Vibrant Buenos Aires

Where tango rhythms meet grand boulevards and mouthwatering steaks

La Boca

Colorful neighborhood famous for its vibrant houses, tango culture, and the iconic Caminito street.

Must-see: Street art and tango performances

Tip: Visit during the day for safety

Recoleta Cemetery

An open-air museum of elaborate mausoleums, including Eva Perón's final resting place.

Hours: 8AM-6PM daily

Nearby: Recoleta cultural center

Teatro Colón

One of the world's top opera houses with perfect acoustics and opulent interiors.

Must-do: Take a guided tour

Performance: Check schedule in advance

Palermo

Trendy neighborhood with parks, boutique shops, and the city's best nightlife.

Areas: Soho, Hollywood, and Viejo

Best for: Dining and shopping

San Telmo Market

Historic Sunday market with antiques, crafts, street performers, and tango dancers.

When: Sundays 10AM-5PM

Find: Unique souvenirs

Argentine Steak

World-famous beef cooked asado style - a must-try culinary experience.

Best cuts: Bife de chorizo or ojo de bife

Pair with: Malbec wine

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Use SUBE card for buses/subte (metro). Taxis are affordable but use ride apps for safety.

Money Matters

Argentine Peso (ARS) is the currency. Bring USD cash for better exchange rates.

Weather

Mild winters (June-Aug), hot summers (Dec-Feb). Spring and fall are ideal.

Language

Spanish is official. English understood in tourist areas but learn basic phrases.
