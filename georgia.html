Where Europe Meets Asia

Discover ancient traditions, breathtaking mountains, and the world's oldest wine culture

Tbilisi

Georgia's vibrant capital blends ancient architecture with modern lifestyle, featuring cobblestone streets, sulfur baths, and a picturesque old town.

Must-see: Narikala Fortress, Old Town, Sulfur Baths

Experience: Traditional Georgian supra (feast)

Kazbegi

Breathtaking mountain region with the iconic Gergeti Trinity Church perched high against the backdrop of Mount Kazbek.

Highlight: Hike to Gergeti Trinity Church

Best time: June to September

Svaneti

Remote mountainous region famous for its medieval defensive towers, unique culture, and some of Georgia's most spectacular landscapes.

Unique feature: Medieval defensive towers

Activities: Trekking, cultural experiences

Kakheti Wine Region

The birthplace of wine, where traditional qvevri (clay pot) winemaking methods have been practiced for over 8,000 years.

Must-try: Wine tasting in family wineries

Famous for: Saperavi and Rkatsiteli wines

Batumi

Georgia's vibrant Black Sea resort city known for its dramatic architecture, botanical gardens, and bustling seaside boulevard.

Attractions: Batumi Boulevard, Alphabet Tower

Specialty: Adjarian khachapuri (cheese bread)

Georgian Cuisine

A feast for the senses with flavorful dishes like khinkali (dumplings), khachapuri (cheese bread), and rich stews, accompanied by polyphonic singing.

Must-try: Khinkali, Khachapuri, Churchkhela

Tradition: Supra (feast) with Tamada (toastmaster)

Georgian Wisdom

"Georgia is a land of ancient traditions and warm hospitality, where guests are considered blessings from God. From the snow-capped peaks of the Caucasus to the sunny Black Sea coast, this country offers dramatic landscapes at every turn. As the birthplace of wine, Georgian culture revolves around the supra - a feast filled with delicious food, abundant wine, and heartfelt toasts that can last for hours. The Georgian people have maintained their unique alphabet and cultural identity despite centuries of foreign influence, creating a destination that feels both European and Asian, yet distinctly its own."

Essential Travel Tips

Visa Requirements

Citizens of 98 countries can visit Georgia visa-free for up to one year. Check specific requirements based on your nationality.

Currency

Georgian Lari (GEL). Credit cards widely accepted in cities, but carry cash for rural areas.

Best Seasons

May-June and September-October for pleasant weather. July-August for mountain hiking, December-March for skiing.

Transportation

Efficient marshrutka (minibus) system connects cities. Renting a car offers flexibility for exploring rural areas.
