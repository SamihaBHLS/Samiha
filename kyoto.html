Experience Timeless Kyoto

Where ancient temples meet cherry blossoms and tea ceremonies

Kinkaku-ji

The Golden Pavilion, covered in gold leaf, reflecting beautifully on its mirror pond in all seasons.

Must-see: Autumn colors reflection

Best time: Early morning to avoid crowds

Fushimi Inari

Famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates winding through the forested hills.

Duration: 2-3 hours for full hike

Tip: Visit at sunrise for magical light

Arashiyama

Bamboo forest and scenic district with temples, river cruises, and monkey park.

Must-do: Sagano Romantic Train

Seasonal: Cherry blossoms in spring

Kiyomizu-dera

Iconic wooden temple with panoramic views and the Otowa Waterfall with sacred waters.

Special: Night illuminations

Viewpoint: Main wooden stage

Gion District

Kyoto's famous geisha district with preserved machiya houses and teahouses.

Must-see: Hanami-koji street

Evening: Spot geiko and maiko

Nishiki Market

"Kyoto's Kitchen" - 400-year-old covered market with local specialties and street food.

Must-try: Tako tamago (candied octopus)

Hours: 9am-6pm (some close Wed)

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Buses cover most attractions. Get a one-day pass. Trains connect major areas.

Money Matters

Japanese Yen (¥) is the currency. Carry cash as many places don't accept cards.

Best Seasons

Spring (cherry blossoms) and autumn (colors) are stunning but crowded.

Temple Etiquette

Remove shoes where required. Be respectful. No photos in restricted areas.
